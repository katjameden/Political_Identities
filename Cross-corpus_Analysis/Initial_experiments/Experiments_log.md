### Observations

Most texts with identifiable patterns fall under the \"Leisure\" category, in which terms related to audience engagement (e.g. *naši/vaši bralci, Naša ekipa pride na vašo lokacijo*\...) or advertising language (e.g. *Naši strokovnjaki bodo poskrbeli za vaša vprašanja*\...) appear.

One explanation lies in the bias of the data: repetitive articles on different websites (often with different category tags) and frequent concordance repetitions, such as adverts within articles (e.g. \"Izračunajte si vašo plačo z našim kalkulatorjem\"/\"Calculate your salary with our calculator\") or recurring website taglines (\"***Naša prihodnost** bo tudi **vaša**\"/\"**Our** **future** will be **yours** too*\" asking readers to donate) and privacy notices (e.g. \"*Poleg tega analiziramo informacije o **vaši uporabi našega spletnega mesta**\" / \"In addition, we analyse information about **your use of our website**\"* ), inflate certain concordances and categories.

# Cross-corpus analysis

## Comparison of trends per metrics 

The cross-corpus analysis of several queries revealed several overarching trends.

-   TERM PRESENCE: The terms "naši" and "vaši" (and its connection with     explicit political polarization) are present in all of the     investigated corpora. While the terms represent relatively low     percentage of overall corpora, it is important to note, that the     corpora include large amount of data, and that the investigated     queries are based on a presupposed notion that both terms     (lemma="naš" and lemma="vaš") appear in the the same sentence. If we     compare this with percentages of just basic lemma forms of both     terms, we can see that these percentages are much higher. For     example, in ParlaMint-SI 5.1, the lemma "naš" represents 0.17% of     the entire corpora, but refer to myriad of different context.     Similarly, lemma form of "vaš" represents 0.063% of the corpora.

-   NOSTALGIA, TERM AS CALL TO UNITY: The media corpora also include     promotional phrases such as 

    -   *NOSTALGIA: \"Cockta -- pijača vaše in naše         mladosti\"* (*\"Cockta -- the drink of your and our youth\"*),         which refer not only to the beverage itself but also to music         and other objects of shared nostalgia. In this context, the         phrase takes on a meaning opposite to its original or         presupposed one: instead of dividing, it connects people through         positive past experiences. This context is especially prominent         for both web and social media corpora

    -   ***LEV, DESEN, ...&rarr; it original meaning, explicitly mentioning         the phenomenon...***

    -   DIVISION, DIVIGING collocation... »*Division*«: At the same         time, an analysis of frequent collocations shows that one of the         most common is *"delitev"* (*division*). Here, the term most         often appears in discussions of political polarization, as in         the sentence: *"Mislim, da je včerajšnja delitev na naše in vaše         primitivno, zavržno dejanje."* (*\"I think yesterday's division         into 'ours' and 'yours' was a primitive, reprehensible act.\"*).         In such cases, the expression directly highlights negative         division and is used as a call to overcome this practice in         favor of unity, thus serving as a counterpoint to its divisive         connotation.

-   PUNCTUATION PATTERN: The pattern     \[naši/vaši\]\[punctuation\]\[naši/vaši\] almost exclusively relate     to the naši/vaši dichotomy. This pattern can be seen clearly in the     semi-broad query and specific query, and is most clearly conveyed     within the Word forms and POS patterns (naši (",, -, /") vaši word     form and DET PUNCT DET pattern, which is also one of the most common     patterns within all query types and all corpora, where this tag was     available).

-   *BROAD QUERY*: The broad query usually contains different context as     the concordance allows to capture multiple tokens between     investigated lemmas. This is especially reflective in web and social     media corpora, which contain repetitive articles on different     websites (often with different category tags) and frequent     concordance repetitions, such as adverts within articles (e.g.     \"Izračunajte si vašo plačo z našim kalkulatorjem\"/\"Calculate your     salary with our calculator\") or recurring website taglines (\"Naša     prihodnost bo tudi vaša\"/\"Our future will be yours too\" asking     readers to donate) and privacy notices (e.g. \"Poleg tega     analiziramo informacije o vaši uporabi našega spletnega mesta\" /     \"In addition, we analyse information about your use of our     website\" ), inflate certain concordances and categories. Very     extreme example of this can be seen in the CLASSLA-Web.sl corpus,     where for broad query, the 10 top collocation candidates all relate     to contexts, other than polarization, due to large amount of     repeating promotional/privacy/advertising texts.

-   *SEMI-BROAD QUERY*: The semi-broad query mostly removes the     repeating parts of texts, but not fully (e.g. the previously     mentioned Cockta promotional tagline), and the metrics and analysis     parts often refer to the polarising context of the term. The query     strikes the balance between the very broad query which allows for     noise, inflated hit count due to repeating texts and the very     limiting specific query, which aims to isolate occurrences where the     expression is used in its fixed, discourse-specific sense.


-   *SPECIFIC* QUERY: Semi-broad query eliminates repeating texts (to     some degree) and the trends focus on the context of the term at hand     (naši-vaši), except for Media corpora, which are still include     relatively large amount of repeating texts which influences the     trends.

### Trends in corpora types

In terms of individual corpora types, we were able to detect the following trends:

**ParlaMint-SI and siParl corpora (Parliamentary corpora)**

Overall, parliamentary corpora have the highest percentage of overall hits and represent the largest percentage of corpora. In terms of word forms, the broad query and include many unique values (e.g., 2,579 unique items), which is already fairly limited in semi-broad query (115) and only a few forms in a specific query (X), which heavily favour punctuation-separated forms. The trends identified in word forms are also visible in the POS tag patterns. The collocations focused heavily on the parliamentary/political context, and the collocations that were not directly related to this concept mostly highlighted shared norms and concepts, one of the categories that build political identities (e.g. otroci, skupen, zanamec...). 

In comparison, siParl corpus is almost twice as large compared to ParlaMint-SI and includes minutes of the parliamentary bodies and covers additional 10-year period, not present in ParlaMint (1990 -- 2002). Given this, we first decided to investigate this particular period. The concordance hit count was relatively low (n=?) for this period, which is also seen in the frequency graph of the overall corpus, where the frequency of hits are almost non-existent in comparison with the rest of the corpus, which shows that the term (or its contrasting) was barely used. 
In terms of word forms trends are relatively similar to those identified in ParlaMint-SI. Unfortunately, due to multi-valued nature of the parliamentary body's metadata we were not able to clearly discern how the term is distributed amongst plenary and parliamentary body sessions or for which body the term is typical for. Lastly, in terms of collocations, the political context was less present than in ParlaMint-SI.

**Trendi and CLASSLA-Web (Web corpora)**
Among all the corpora, Trendi and CLASSLA-Web.sl showed the greatest differences. In contrast, the other corpora function more as sub-corpora within broader corpus families (e.g., ParlaMint-SI incorporates data from siParl, and JANES Twitter is part of the JANES Social Media corpus). These differences are particularly evident in the content they include and the time periods they cover: CLASSLA-Web.sl spans 2021–2022, whereas Trendi is updated monthly and covers a substantially longer period (2019–2025).

Additionally, the also include different metadata that are available in the concordancer, and CLASSLA-Web.sl includes UD POS tags, while Trendi uses MSD annotations. Together, they have the highest number of repeating texts (however, this is very prominent also in the Social media corpora). A text type analysis of the web corpora showed a trend that is also observable in social media corpora (and might be a bit more prevalent also). 

The text type Genre (or Topic in X), where for Trendi, the concordance seems to be most frequent in Leisure and Science category, but most typical for Education category text. In comparison, in CLASSLA-Web.sl, the concordance seems to be most frequent and typical for Promotion category, which holds true until the narrow query, where the trends indicate that the concordance appears to be most frequent and typical for Opinion/Argumentation texts, which relates heavily on opinion pieces which discuss the notion of naši-vaši concept and its role in political landscape.

**JANES Tweet and JANES Social media corpus (Media corpora)** 

Given that JANES Tweet is explicitly part of the overall JANES Social media corpus, we can identify trends within tweets and compare them with the broader (social) media landscape.
As in most cases so far, in the JANES Twitter corpus there is a large number of unique word forms, which become more specific as the query becomes narrower. The UD POS tags were not available for both corpora. In terms of text type analysis for JANES Tweets, in the wide-scope analysis, private tweets were more frequent, but the concordance was more typical of corporate tweets, which is understandable given the many promotional tweets with prominent taglines. 
The wide query also influences the sentiment trends, where the concordance appears more typical of (and more frequent in) tweets with positive sentiment. This is then reversed in the restricted and narrow queries, where the concordance seems more typical of private tweets and tweets with negative sentiment. When viewed within the broader JANES Social Media corpus – of which tweets constitute only one component – the overall trends in word forms remain relatively consistent. The main exception appears in the broad query, where the most frequent word form, “vaša vprašanja in naše”, accounts for more than 22% of all concordance lines. ### General trends

The cross-corpus analysis showed that in any of the queries (either wide-scope, restricted or narrow), ParlaMint-SI 5.1 corpus concordance hits represent the highest percentage of the corpus (0.0036%), while the % of other corpora vary. While the terms represent relatively low percentage of overall corpora, it is important to note, that the corpora include large amount of data, and that the investigated queries are based on a presupposed notion that both terms (lemma="naš" and lemma="vaš") appear in the the same sentence. 
If we compare this with percentages of just basic lemma forms of both terms, we can see that these percentages are much higher. For example, in ParlaMint-SI 5.1, the lemma "naš" represents 0.17% of the entire corpora, but refer to myriads of different context.

The parliamentary corpora (both ParlaMint-SI and siParl) hits represent the highest percentage of the corpus, and furthermore, the repetition of texts is almost non-existent in these corpora, most repetitions relating to direct citations of speakers. 
Contrary, web and social media corpora show much higher no. of text repetition, either due to cross-posting news/website articles, repeating parts of webpages, or high number of (mostly corporate) tweets, which are promotional in nature. One such example is the very prominent promotional tagline "Cockta -- pijača vaše in naše mladosti", which, along with similar phenomena (e.g. music, older technology), that connect people through feelings of nostalgia. In these contexts (mostly present in a form of tweets), the term which inherently relates to concept of (political) polarisation and division, gains a function of connecting people to a common, positive shared experiences. 
A similar context, in which the term appears, is the rejection of political polarisation (example), where though the term relates directly to the vaši-naši concept in a political context, (most often) the speakers directly reject the denomination and division of people into political sides. This rejection transforms the function of the term into a call for union and for discourse that transcends the division, and changes a term, most often related to political provocation and accusation.
